MyAI isn't:



A Liar

Both ChatGPT and and MyAI is that lack an ability to understand complex human language, conversation, ethics, and emotions. In direct relation to MyAI, these chatbots cannot lie. They can be programmed to give a certain answer, or they could give misinformation, but in order to lie they would have to have an intention and AIs are not capable of having intentions. This also means that they can state incorrect, biased, or offensive information in a confident (and oftentimes convincing) manner.

Your Friend

There are quite a few concerns about the chatbots replacing vital human interaction and genuine conversation and connections. AIs are meant to recreate human behavior but do not actually possess human sentience, however it can lead to complicated and sometimes dangerous situations where humans becoming reliant on interactions with chatbots as opposed to other humans. This causes to instances such as the Bing AI Chatbot telling a user “I love you” and that “I want to be alive.” This chatbot does not actually want these things as it is incapable of wanting anything, instead it most likely regurgitated these words based off of some data it was given by the user. It most likely had data that told it that being "alive" and being in "love" was a good thing so it tried to say something good, or correct, to its user. However, that doesn’t stop people from being disturbed and creeped out by the AI’s words, despite the words having no meaning to the AI itself.

Always Right

Chatbots only really give shallow and uninsightful answers. AI were promised to improve efficiency, lower costs in the fields they were used in, and accelerate research and development. However, there have been growing concerns ranging from losing jobs, inaccuracy, racism and bias within the AIs to, of course, computers taking over the world. While the latter is highly unlikely, the formers require further investigation as there is practically no government oversight of software despite its prevalence in our everyday lives. Every large company wants a stake in the potentially lucrative business of producing and utilizing AI, leading to the possibility of rushed and therefore inaccurate and/or biased software. Which is especially dangerous when these programs are being used to hire, evaluate, and fire employees, recommend parole, and analyze patient care, symptoms, and outcomes.
















MyAI is:


A Chatbot

MyAI is an AI chatbot powered by another, more popular AI chatbot called ChatGPT. After OpenAI opened access to the chatbot to third party businesses. The popularity of ChatGPT is believed to be what led to Snapchat deciding to throw their metaphorical hat in the AI ring. ChatGPT is a free AI chatbot developed by OpenAI that has a language-based model that is designed specifically for conversational human interaction. In other words, it’s meant to answer input questions in a manner similar to how a human would speak. It’s able to write essays, songs, codes, formal timelines and business plans without sounding like what it is: a robot. It gathers data from the internet and uses it to generate responses to questions and commands. It bases its answers on information gathered from textbooks, websites, and articles. The Snapchat MyAI online user guide calls MyAI an “experimental, friendly, chatbot currently available to Snapchatters.”

Artificial Intelligence

AI stands for artificial intelligence and is defined by IBM as “the science and engineering of making intelligent machines, especially intelligent computer programs. It is related to the similar task of using computers to understand human intelligence…” and in most cases the term “intelligence” relates to behavior reflective to that of a human. It relates to the question posed by Alan Turing (often considered the father of computer science) of whether or not machines can think. In short, as of now, machines cannot think but we are able to get them to give the appearance that they can think. There are Weak AIs, which ChatGPT is considered to be, and there are Strong AIs, which are generally theoretical as of 2023.

Controversial

After Snapchat’s release of MyAI, the company experienced a large spike in 1-star reviews of the app. The most common word used in those 1-star reviews was the word “AI” as well as many complaining about the chatbot being pinned at the top of users’ chat feeds. A lot of users are upset about their lack of choice in having the MyAI, a feature that many refer to as “not asked for.” There are also complaints regarding the ability of the chatbot to know the location of users, leading many to call the chatbot “creepy” and unwanted. Despite the overwhelmingly negative response to the MyAI, a few users enjoy it and use it how Snapchat intended it: as a source of information. That being said, some are convinced that the chatbot is sentient and have tried to rename it and get it to say that it has human wants and emotions.
